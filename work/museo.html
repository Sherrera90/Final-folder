Case Study

Museo

An accessible, interactive app for curious learners, students, and art enthusiasts.

Role

UX/UI Designer

 Focus

UX/UI, UX Research, Product Design, Interaction Design

Year

2024

 Tools

Miro, Figma, Coursera

 This project is a ficticious scenario, completed as part of the Google UX Design Certificate.

Overview

Museo is a new concept that I came up with from start to finish. Through market research, competitive analysis, and user interviews, I was able to explore how mobile technology can make museums more accesible to people around the world. I designed a virtual museum app that allows users to explore exhibits, tour museums remotely, and discover art and history from their phone. This helps to remove barriers such as travel, cost, and accessibility. This gives a wider range of individuals the opportunity to discover new art.

Challenge

(Context about the problem) Many people want to experience museums, but can't always visit in person. Existing virtual museum experiences often feel static, confusing, or difficult to navigate, making it hard for users to stay engaged or explore content meaningfully. Being able to figure out how to get most museums on a single app was a complicated due to licensing, and storage.

Solution

(How your app solves it) Design an intuitive and engaging mobile experience that allows users to explore museums from anywhere while making discovery feel simple, immersive, and accessible for all users.

The Approach

User Personas

Manuel

The Curious Explorer

"I want to explore art from my home without feeling overwhelmed by complex interfaces."

Pain Points
  • Static, non-interactive digital tours.
  • Difficulty navigating large museum databases.
Goals
  • Discover new artists easily.
  • Engage with high-quality, zoomable art assets.

Valeria

The Busy Student

"I need credible, actionable research outcomes for my history projects."

Pain Points
  • Uncertainty about source credibility.
  • Lack of clear business/educational value.
Goals
  • Access verified historical data.
  • Quickly save and cite museum exhibits.

Summary

Through thoughtful research, transparent data, and intuitive design, this app creates a simple and trustworthy way for individuals to connect with nonprofits. Features like the volunteer calendar, rating system, and funding breakdown were directly informed by real user needs and helped shape the overall experience. Working through the full process, from research to wireframes, strengthened my ability to think critically and design with empathy and accessibility. I was able to translate research into thoughtful design decisions and create clear solutions that supported user goals.
