Case Study

Museo

An accessible, interactive app for curious learners, students, and art enthusiasts.

Role — UX/UI Designer Focus — UX/UI, UX Research, Product Design, Interaction Design Year — 2024 Tools — Miro, Figma, Coursera This project is a ficticious scenario, completed as part of the Google UX Design Certificate.
02

Overview

Museo is a new concept that I came up with from start to finish. Through market research, competitive analysis, and user interviews, I was able to explore how mobile technology can make museums more accesible to people around the world. I designed a virtual museum app that allows users to explore exhibits, tour museums remotely, and discover art and history from their phone. This helps to remove barriers such as travel, cost, and accessibility. This gives a wider range of individuals the opportunity to discover new art.

Challenge

Many people want to experience museums, but can't always visit in person. Existing virtual museum experiences often feel static, confusing, or difficult to navigate, making it hard for users to stay engaged or explore content meaningfully. Being able to figure out how to get most museums on a single app was a complicated due to licensing, and storage.

Goals

Design an intuitive and engaging mobile experience that allows users to explore museums from anywhere while making discovery feel simple, immersive, and accessible for all users.

The Approach

1. API Integration

  • Pulled live data for temperation, conditions, "feels like", wind speed, and humidity.
  • Added an hourly forecast with weather icons.

2. UI & Retro Design

  • Gradient style heading with a search bar and pixel-inspired buttons.
  • Use of popular squiggle design often seen on disposable cups in the 90's/
  • Pixel inspired typography and bold neon highlight text.
  • Playful weather icons with a cartoon vibe.

3. User Experience

  • Added the ability to easily search for your city.
  • Large centered temperature and condition that is easily seen.
  • Hourly forecase displayed in a timeline-style with icons and temperatures below it.

Summary

The final design offers live, accurate weather updates in a UI that feels nostalgic and fun. By leaning into 90's web nostalgia, the app stands apart from stand weather apps, offering a functional tool that is wrapped in a visually exciting experience. While showcasing my experience in API calls, responsive CSS and DOM manipulation.
