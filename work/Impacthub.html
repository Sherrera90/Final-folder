Case Study

ImpactHub

A platform designed to help people discover, trust, and support nonprofits through transparency, and privacy while allowing users to make confident decisions.

Role — UX/UI Designer Focus — UX Strategy, UI Systems Year — 2025 Tools — Miro, Whimsical, Procreate, Figma This project is a ficticious scenario, completed as part of Entrylevel's UX Design program

Secondary research: Identifying patterns in App Store sentiment.

Overview

ImpactHub is an platform for both individuals who are looking to volunteer and non-profits looking for volunteers. The platform and app provide a private space for the user to explore on their own, and gives the organizations a chance to expand their volunteer base. This helps to simplify onboarding, boost engagement, track hours from both sides, all while giving the users and in-depth looking into the individuals and charities available.

Challenge

There are many websites out there that are designed to help users find places to volunteer, but they lack functionality, discoverability, or inconsistent information. Users asked for full disclosure regarding the organization, their policies, and where their funds are being alllocated. They also requested volunteer tracking abilities, and organization features like a calendar and hours tracked.

Goals

The goal was to create a website and mobile app that was functional, and easy to use while addressing all of the needs of our users. optimizing the user interface for both large and small screens, creating functionality that transitions between devices, and enchancing accessibility to a variety of users.

The Approach

Users, stakeholders, and their needs

  • I conducted numerous interviews to gain a better understanding of users & their needs.

User Flows

  • We mapped out the stages they go through and the tasks that get performed at each stage.

Building the experience

  • I drew early iteration ideas based on the user journeys and the data I gathered. Through all of that, I was able to generate proofs through Figma and Whimsical.

Summary

Through thoughtful research, transparent data, and intuitive design, this app creates a simple and trustworthy way for individuals to connect with nonprofits. Features like the volunteer calendar, rating system, and funding breakdown were directly informed by real user needs and helped shape the overall experience. Working through the full process, from research to wireframes, strengthened my ability to think critically and design with empathy and accessibility. I was able to translate research into thoughtful design decisions and create clear solutions that supported user goals.
